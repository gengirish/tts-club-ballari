Sister Stride

Steel Sisters & Striders — SSS Club Ballari · Member & visitor guide

This guide explains who the app is for, what you can do as a member or visitor, and how the main areas of the product fit together. Sister Stride is the digital home for a women-first fitness community in and around Ballari, Karnataka, India.

1. Who this is built for

  • Women in the Ballari area who want structured support for walking, running, strength, habits, and wellness.
  • Members who track progress, join challenges, follow programmes (including Couch to 5K / Walking to 5K), sign up for club events, and connect in the community feed.
  • Coaches and hosts (when your club assigns those roles) who use separate desks in the same product to support sessions and events.

Dates and day-to-day activity in the app are oriented to India time (IST) where you see local dates and “today” for logging.

2. Getting started

Sign in or join

  • Open the club’s web address in your browser and use Sign in.
  • You can sign in with email and password, username and password, or (where the club has enabled it) email magic link or mobile OTP via WhatsApp.
  • New to the club? Use Join on the sign-in page to create an account with email and/or username and a password.
  • Forgot your password? Use Forgot password? on the sign-in page to get a reset link by email (you need an email on your account).

First-time member setup

After you first sign in, you may be guided through onboarding: your profile, health basics, activity level, and goals. Complete this so your home dashboard and fitness score can work fully.

3. Your home dashboard

After onboarding, Home gives you a quick picture of your day and what matters next:

  • Steps today — visual progress toward a daily step goal.
  • Fitness score — a club score based on your profile and recent logs (see the Score section).
  • Weight snapshot — from your health profile or today’s progress log.
  • Walking to 5K — shortcut to register or update your flagship walking programme intake.
  • Shortcuts to next events, your active challenge, and other quick links where the club has data for you.

From Home you can open Edit profile to change details and goals later.

4. Main member areas

Area What you can do
Score See your fitness score level and breakdown. Recompute refreshes your score from your latest health profile and progress (when you have enough data).
Progress Log steps, weight, water, sleep, walks, runs, workouts for “today” (IST). View trends over recent weeks and months. Saving updates your dashboard and can feed your fitness score.
Challenges Browse active club challenges (for example step challenges). Join a challenge, see your place on the leaderboard, and stay motivated with the group.
Events See upcoming club sessions (walks, runs, yoga, meetups, and more). Register for a spot, open Details for time and place, and check in when you arrive at the venue.
C25K (Couch to 5K) Explore the 12-week Couch to 5K programme: phases, session plan, strength notes, safety, and (when offered) paid enrolment through the club’s payment flow. Links to Walking to 5K registration for the full intake.
Coaches Browse coaches the club lists (running, walking, strength, yoga, nutrition, physio, and more). Book session
Community Read wellness articles picked for the community. Post short updates, like and comment on others’ posts. Use SOS for an urgent safety alert if the club has wired it to their support channel.

5. Walking to 5K registration (visitors & members)

Walking to 5K is the club’s structured path into the flagship walking programme, aligned with Couch to 5K inside the app.

  • From the public site you can open Walking to 5K registration to create an account (if needed) and complete a step-by-step wizard: contact details, health screening (PAR-Q style questions), emergency contact, and agreements.
  • If you already have an account, sign in first; the wizard remembers you are registering for the programme.
  • After you are enrolled, programme reminders may be sent by WhatsApp or email when the club has turned those integrations on.

6. Events — what to expect

  • Events show type (walk, run, yoga, meetup, cycling, trekking), time in IST, location, and host name when available.
  • Capacity may be shown; when an event is full, registration closes for new joiners unless the host opens more spots.
  • After you register, use Check in on the event page when you are physically at the session so the host has an accurate roster.
  • Some clubs use a separate guest or paid registration link for special events; follow the link your host shares.

7. Install as an app (optional)

The site can behave like an app on your phone: install or Add to Home Screen from your browser menu when your club’s site supports it (Progressive Web App). You get a home-screen icon and quicker access; you still sign in securely through the web.

8. Coaches, hosts, and admins (short overview)

Most readers use Member mode. Other roles exist so the same product can run the club end-to-end:

  • Coach — a desk to see members and programme enrolments relevant to coaching.
  • Host — create and manage events, review registrations, and support check-in.
  • Admin — club-wide overview, member directory tools, and operational panels (for example OTP or notification issues).

You only see these areas if your account has been given that role.

9. Privacy, safety, and support

  • Sign out with Log out when you finish on a shared device.
  • Community posts should stay respectful and on-topic; the club may moderate according to their rules.
  • SOS is for genuine urgent situations when the club has connected it to a monitored inbox or process — not for general questions.
  • For account problems, wrong phone number, or payment questions, contact your club organisers or the support channel they advertise.
